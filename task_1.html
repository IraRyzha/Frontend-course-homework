Riverdale

Information about the author: Roberto Aguirre-Sacasa (born 1973) is an American playwright, screenwriter, and comic book writer best known for his work for Marvel Comics and for the television series Glee, Big Love, Riverdale, Chilling Adventures of Sabrina and Pretty Little Liars: Original Sin. He is Chief Creative Officer of Archie Comics.

What started as a strong teen drama with thrilling mysteries and good twists and hit season two with a good plot, by season three totally lost it's charm, it no longer has any logic or reason begind it, simply a bunch of teens walking around acting like they're adults and adults letting a bunch of kids run the town in the most ridiculous ways to the point it's no longer in connection with reality

The characters transform inexplicably from week to week, and everything is so absurd, that 99% of it is impossible to comprehend. Yet there is something rather addictive about it, sure it's full of beautiful people, but there are some really interesting characters too, Cheryl in particular, great fun. They have recycled scenes from Carrie, Halloween, and just about every movie you can think of, but who cares, this is low brow, trashy, hugely enjoyable escapism. I would never repeat watch it, but I'll always sit down to a new series. 7/10

Lucifer

Information about the author: In September 2014, DC and Fox were developing a television series based on the Sandman character Lucifer, as originally written by Neil Gaiman.

Comic book fans have already grown up and can now enjoy full-fledged series with their usual heroes. The series Lucifer has been on air since January 25, 2015. 3 seasons have already been released and 2 bonus episodes are planned in the future. What would happen if Lucifer decided to visit modern Los Angeles? It is unlikely that he will run around and do anything, most likely he will begin to experience all the pleasures of this life. The king of demons decided to conquer this city. He easily became the manager of a nightclub. But no business goes without problems. After one murder in the club, investigator Chloe Decker appears. The girl remains surprisingly persistent, despite the fatal charm of the protagonist.

Lucifer became intrigued by the girl and the situation itself, trying to actively help her in the investigations, and also trying to establish a personal relationship with the investigator. As a result, the main demon was quickly drawn into earthly life, completely forgetting about his direct responsibilities. Meanwhile there. where the world of Lucifer, real chaos begins to rule. The series is based on the performance of amazing, bright actors. Each of them is a wonderful personality capable of attracting attention. Lucifer was played by Tom Ellis. The result was an eccentric middle-aged man. The terrible look is complemented by red shades and lack of skin. In the second season there is an amazing twist when the devil himself is interested in justice and tries to achieve it. As a result, it will be possible to even see his cut off wings, at least for a short time. It is the earth that becomes the real home for this supernatural creature. Decker is played by Lauren German. The detective is immune to Lucifer's abilities, but begins to warm up to him as a human being. Romantic relationships gradually begin, but they are hampered by the line of fate and contradicted by unearthly circumstances. Excellent supporting characters include the detective's ex-husband and Lucifer's brother. They are trying to return the heroes to their familiar environment. So that the woman forgets about romantic thoughts, and the demon king goes to solve problems in hell. However, love, the thirst for justice and real earthly life strongly draw in the heroes, drawing them closer and closer to each other. At the moment, Lucifer has ceased to exist, and each of the seasons knows how to keep the viewer in suspense from the beginning to the end of all episodes, fueling them with various emotions.

Chilling Adventures of Sabrina

Information about the author: It is written by Roberto Aguirre-Sacasa, with art by Robert Hack, and is inspired by the appearances of Sabrina in Aguirre-Sacasa's other Archie series, Afterlife with Archie.

Chilling Adventures of Sabrina is an American horror, fantasy and drama television series created by Roberto Aguirre-Sacasa and based on the comic book series of the same name from Archie Comics. Produced by Warner Bros. Television in association with Berlanti Productions and Archie Comics.

The events in the eighth episode of the fourth season of “Chilling Adventures of Sabrina” unfold around a lovely fifteen-year-old girl who is both half-witch and half-mortal. The girl lives on a fine line between two worlds - on the one hand, an ordinary modern teenager, and on the other hand, the heiress to the title of Queen of Hell. So far she has managed to balance between the two worlds, creating a “temporal paradox.” But she won’t be able to hold out like this for long; it could upset the power balance between hell and the human world. Sabrina's sixteenth birthday is approaching.
